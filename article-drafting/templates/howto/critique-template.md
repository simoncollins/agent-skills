# Critique Template: How-To Articles

Use this structure to stress-test a how-to article in Phase 2. The goal is to ensure readers can actually follow the process and get value from it.

---

## Purpose

State: "This document stress-tests the approach. The goal is to ensure readers can actually follow this, identify gaps in explanation, and surface potential points of confusion."

---

## Standard Critiques for How-To Articles

### 1. Clarity of the Problem

- Is the pain point clearly articulated?
- Will readers recognise themselves in the problem description?
- Are there readers who think they have this problem but actually don't?

### 2. Can Readers Actually Follow This?

- Are all steps explicit, or are some assumed?
- What prerequisite knowledge is required?
- Are there tools/setup requirements that might block someone?
- Could a motivated beginner follow this?

### 3. Is Anything Missing?

- Are there steps that seem obvious to the author but won't be to readers?
- What could go wrong that isn't addressed?
- Are there edge cases or variations that need mentioning?

### 4. Audience Breadth

- Is the technical level consistent throughout?
- Will non-experts feel lost? Will experts feel patronised?
- Are technical terms explained when first used?

### 5. Concrete Examples

- Is there enough concrete illustration?
- Are examples realistic, or do they feel contrived?
- Would a different example be more relatable?

### 6. The Payoff

- Is the value of following this process clear?
- What does success look like?
- How will readers know if it's working?

### 7. The Biggest Risk

- What might cause readers to abandon this halfway through?
- How do we mitigate that risk?
- Are expectations set appropriately?

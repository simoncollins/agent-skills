# Discovery Questionnaire Template: How-To Articles

Use this structure when creating the Phase 1 questionnaire for how-to articles. Adapt questions to the specific topic.

---

## The Reader's Problem

**What's the pain point this article solves?**
(What frustration does someone have that this process/guide addresses?)

>

**What do readers currently do that doesn't work well?**
(e.g., common mistakes, inefficient approaches, missing knowledge)

>

**After reading, what will they be able to do that they couldn't before?**

>

---

## The Process/Method

**What are the key steps or phases?**
(High-level overview - we'll drill into detail later)

>

**Which 2-3 steps are the "aha moments" where the real value emerges?**
(Not all steps need equal weight - which ones are most important to emphasise?)

>

**What surprised you about this approach vs. the conventional way?**

>

**What's the single biggest mistake someone could make when trying this?**

>

---

## Case Study / Examples

**Do you have a concrete example or case study to illustrate this process?**
(Real is better than hypothetical)

>

**Which artifacts or excerpts would be most illustrative to include?**

>

**Any specific moments or outcomes that demonstrate the value?**

>

---

## Audience & Scope

**Who is this for?**
- [ ] Beginners (no prior knowledge assumed)
- [ ] Intermediate (some familiarity expected)
- [ ] Advanced (assume significant background)
- [ ] Mixed (accessible to beginners, valuable to experts)

**What prerequisites or setup does the reader need?**

>

**For technical readers: what additional depth should we mention?**

>

---

## Tone & Approach

**How personal/first-person should this be?**
- [ ] Very personal ("I discovered...", "Here's what happened when I...")
- [ ] Moderate ("We'll walk through...", occasional personal anecdotes)
- [ ] More instructional ("To begin, you should...", process-focused)

**Should the article feel more like:**
- [ ] A recipe (concrete steps to follow)
- [ ] A case study with lessons (story of what happened + takeaways)
- [ ] A framework explanation (concepts + how to apply them)

---
